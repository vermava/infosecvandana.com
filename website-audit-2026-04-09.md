Date: 2026-04-09

## Summary

Your site already captures a lot of strong historical material, including talks, webinars, awards, podcasts, and media coverage through 2024. The biggest gaps are:

- the homepage bio is stale and still centers older roles/focus areas
- the site does not yet reflect your stronger public positioning around AI security, LLM security, MCP security, and software supply chain security

### Bio wording to refresh

Suggested direction for the homepage/about page:

- Keep the broad background across AppSec, cloud, product security, vulnerability management, and security leadership.
- Update the present-tense focus to AI security, secure AI-native development, AppSec, software supply chain security, and developer security advocacy.
- Update current role wording carefully because public sources are inconsistent:
  - Snyk contributor page title says `Senior Developer Advocate, Snyk`
  - the same page body says `Security Relations Leader at Snyk`
- Update OWASP wording carefully:
  - newer sources show `Vice Chair` / `Member` wording
  - older sources on your site still say `Chair`
  - you may want to choose the exact current wording you prefer and use that consistently everywhere

### Add a recent highlights block to the homepage

Pull the newest 4-6 entries from:

- `talks`
- `webinars`
- `awards`
- `featured`

This will immediately surface newer work without forcing visitors to browse every section.

### Add an upcoming section

You now have at least one clear upcoming public item from 2026:

- OWASP Italy Day 2026 training and keynote

An `upcoming` or `now` section would help keep current activity visible.

### Normalize metadata

Some content files use:

- plain reference URLs in body text
- no `featureimage`
- inconsistent title formatting
- inconsistent role wording

It would help to standardize:

- `title`
- `date`
- `featureimage`
- `external_url`
- `summary`
- `event_name`
- `location`
- `status` such as `past` or `upcoming`

- contact links
